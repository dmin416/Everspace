# Glossary

Canonical terms for the **Story/** rewrite. When writing or editing `Story/` chapters, use these names. The **Novel/** source may use different terms (e.g. Enel, Krishna, Stella Online). Do not carry novel terminology into the story rewrite.

---

# CREDITS

**Credits** are the galaxy-wide electronic currency in the Story rewrite.

| Use this | Not this (in Story/) |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Credits** | Enel, Enels, credits |
| **zero Credits** | zero Enel |
| **5,000 Credits** | 5,000 Enel |

- Always write **Credits** with a capital **C**
- D may think or say *No Credits either* and similar. That is correct
- **Never use Enel in Story/** prose. The novel uses Enel. The story uses **Credits**
- Credits are tracked electronically. Unlogged trades often use **rare materials** or barter instead

---

# RARE MATERIALS

**Rare materials** are valuable raw and refined resources used for crafting and off-books trade.

| Use this | Not this (in Story/) |
|----------|----------------------|
| **rare materials** | rare metals, rare metal, materials (when meaning this) |

- Write **rare materials** in lowercase unless it starts a sentence
- The novel source says rare metals. The story says **rare materials**
- Ores, crystals, ingots and salvage all count as rare materials when D is talking generally

---

# TRAVEL

How ships move and arrive. Full premise in [`travel.md`](travel.md).

| Term | Use in Story/ |
|------|----------------|
| **dropping out of warp** | Hostiles or traffic arriving in the sector. AI warning format, not a scan |
| **warp in** / **warped in** | Same arrival beat. More casual phrasing |
| **supralight** | Fast travel between locations inside a system |
| **jump gate** / **wormhole** | Standard hops between star systems |
| **boost** | Short combat sprint. Default dogfight speed-up |
| **Cruise Drive** | Long charged sprint inside a sector when the path is clear |

- **Never describe the initial contact as a scan.** The Orca warns about **ships dropping out of warp**
- Loitering in one sector long enough eventually brings hostiles. See [`travel.md`](travel.md)
