# Crafting

Premise reference for the **Story/** rewrite. Based on **Everspace 2** crafting as D knows it from the game. Not a full rules doc. Use this for tone and logic when D handles gear, salvage or materials.

See also [`Glossary.md`](Glossary.md) for **Credits** and [`d-assets.md`](d-assets.md) for ship gear.

---

## Core idea

Gear is not disposable. Bad, outdated or surplus weapons and modules get **dismantled** into **parts**, then **crafted** or **modified** into something useful. That is why scrap, salvage and raw materials matter as much as **Credits**.

D expects this loop everywhere civilized ship work happens: stations, colonies, properly equipped ships with cargo space.

---

## The loop

1. **Dismantle** unwanted equipment → receive rarity-based **parts** (and blueprint progress where applicable)
2. **Unlock blueprints** by looting them or dismantling three items of the same type and rarity
3. **Craft** known gear at the player's current level by spending parts
4. **Improvise** when no blueprint exists: spend parts for a random weapon, secondary or module at a chosen rarity (not set gear or mission items)
5. **Modify** gear you already have: raise rarity or level, remove level locks, install catalysts, reroll bonus stats

Crafted-from-parts items are **Improvised**: they can be dismantled again but their rarity cannot be pushed higher through modification.

---

## Parts (main crafting currency)

Dismantling yields fewer parts than crafting costs. Higher rarity gear breaks down into more valuable part types.

Common part tiers (low → high):

| Part | Role |
|------|------|
| **Circuitry** | Base electronic scrap |
| **Spare Parts** | General mechanical salvage |
| **Power Coupling** | Power routing components |
| **Quantum Processor** | High-end control hardware |
| **Starcharged Conduit** | Top-tier energy channeling |
| **Polymorphic Alloy** | Ascendant-tier exotic material |
| **Organic Deepspace Semiconductor** | Legendary dismantle yield |

**Components** (Supra Gel, Power Cell, Crystal Diode, etc.) sit beside parts: used for consumables, catalysts and perk unlocks. Often crafted from **ores, crystals and basic resources** gathered outside pure dismantling.

**Catalysts** add or change equipment prefixes and performance. Crafted from components. Common recipes are always known. Better catalysts unlock from dangerous areas.

## What can be crafted

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Primary weapons** | Pulse Laser, Beam Laser, Rail Gun, Autocannon, Scatter Gun, Blaster, Thermo Gun, Flak, Gauss Cannon, Coil Gun |
| **Secondary weapons** | Homing, EMP, Shieldbreaker and other missile types, Rockets, Mines |
| **Modules** | Energy Core, Shield, Plating variants, Sensor, Booster, **Cargo Unit** (unlocks crafting on a ship) |
| **Consumables** | Nanobots, injectors, repair kits, drones, cloak fields, resupply packs (crafted as full stacks) |

Variants of base weapons and modules usually require **improvising** at their minimum rarity unless a specific blueprint was unlocked.

---

## Modification (existing gear)

Only while the user's level is within two levels of the item.

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Increase rarity** | One tier up. Cannot be done on Improvised crafted items. Superior and Legendary are capped |
| **Increase level** | One level at a time, up to the user's level |
| **Remove level restriction** | Equip higher-level gear early |
| **Install catalyst** | Add or overwrite a prefix. One catalyst at a time |
| **Re-roll bonus attributes** | New random modifiers. Legendary cannot reroll |

All modifications spend parts. Costs scale with item rarity.

## Story tie-ins

- **Valuable rare materials** in cargo (ores, crystals, refined stock) feed crafting and off-books trade when **Credits** are logged or unavailable
- D treats a ship with a **Cargo Unit** and a stash of parts as self-sufficient. Without them he is buying, looting or grinding
- Salvage after combat is worth keeping: even junk gear becomes parts and blueprint progress
- **Improvised** gear is a stopgap. D knows it is fine for the moment but not worth investing modification into
- Crafting is a normal part of mercenary life, not a rare miracle. Colonies and stations that serve pilots expect dismantle bays and fabricators
